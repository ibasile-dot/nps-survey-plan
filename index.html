NPS & Organiser Feedback — Full Plan

Owner: Ilaria Basile  |  Updated: 10 April 2026  |  Epic: EP-50795  |  Google Sheet Dashboard  |  Status: In Progress

1. What we're solving

No structured feedback loop from organisers. No NPS baseline. No comparable data across clients. Post-event surveys sent by email historically failed (low response). We need measurable data that feeds product decisions, retention strategy, and OKR tracking.

2. What we agreed (March 18 & April 10)

  • Structured surveys at key points in the event cycle — same questions across all clients for comparable data
  • RM-led — clients are more honest with RMs than TAMs. RM sends the link; if not completed in 7 days, walks through it on the next call
  • Questions agreed with the product team — product-specific insights that drive improvements
  • Surveys sit in PlanHat as tasks per event — RMs adjust dates or skip with a reason. Flexible, not automated
  • Typeform for collection (Mykyta approved), Google Sheets for analysis, PlanHat for tracking

3. Three surveys

Based on Barry and Oriana's feedback (April 10): merged the original onboarding and pre-event surveys (the overlap was too large for first-time clients), and reframed the renewal survey as an annual review focused on the overall relationship — not tied to renewal timing. Each survey: 5 questions, ~3 minutes.

#SurveyWhenPurposeTypeform
S1Setup & Configuration2-4 weeks after kickoff / once organiser has started configuringCaptures onboarding experience, self-service level, platform friction areas. Critical for new clients — shows we're proactive and listening (Barry).Open survey
S2Post-EventBetween event end and debrief/wash-up callCore survey. NPS, overall satisfaction, product and service feedback. Feeds into the debrief call structure — results can be discussed during the wash-up.Open survey
S3Annual ReviewOnce per year — RM decides timing based on relationshipStrategic view: platform trajectory, investment priorities, competitive gaps. Positioned as understanding the overall relationship, not a renewal conversation.Open survey
Why 3 surveys, not 4: Barry and Oriana confirmed that for first-time clients, onboarding and pre-event are the same phase. Merging avoids survey fatigue. The annual review is separate because those strategic questions don't fit naturally right after an event — they need distance and a broader perspective.

5. Two plans: one-event vs multi-event clients

Not one-size-fits-all — the survey cycle depends on the client's event portfolio. Both plans use the same 3 surveys but with different timing and frequency.

6. Delivery flow

  1. PlanHat task — Based on event date, PlanHat creates a task for the RM. RM can adjust the date or skip with a reason.
  2. RM sends Typeform link — Via email after the relevant call or milestone.
  3. 7-day window — If not completed, PlanHat reminds the RM.
  4. Fallback — RM walks through the survey on the next call (screen share, first 5 minutes).
  5. Data flows automatically — Typeform → Google Sheet → Dashboard. No manual entry.

RM Strategy Call Playbook

Best-practice conversation prompts for strategy calls. Not a script — a resource to draw from when it fits the conversation. Barry described this as a "relationship management playbook" that future RMs can also use.

These are different from the survey questions. The survey captures scores. These go deeper — conversational follow-ups that give context behind the numbers.

StageSurvey asks (scores)Call goes deeper (conversation)
Setup / OnboardingHow easy was it to get started? (1-10)
  • Walk me through how you approached the setup — what did you tackle first?
  • When you got stuck, what did you do? Who did you go to?
  • What did you expect the platform to do that it didn't?
Pre-EventHow satisfied so far? (1-10)
  • What's your biggest worry about the event going live?
  • Tell me about the last time you had to contact support — what happened?
  • If you had an extra hour this week, what would you spend it on?
Post-EventWould you recommend us? (NPS 0-10)
  • What moment during the event made you think "this works" or "this doesn't"?
  • What feedback did you get from your own attendees or exhibitors?
  • If you ran this event again tomorrow, what would you change on our side?
Annual / RelationshipHas platform gotten better/same/worse?
  • What's the conversation internally about the platform — what do your colleagues say?
  • What's the one thing that would make this decision easy for you?
  • Is there a tool or workflow outside our platform that you wish was inside it?
