1. Context & Problem

We have no structured, recurring feedback loop from organisers. Post-event surveys historically fail (clients don't fill them). The March 18 Product & Business sync agreed on structured surveys during existing calls, but the format, questions, and tooling are undefined. Meanwhile, Ilaria is mapping the end-to-end organiser journey and needs real organiser data — not just TAM proxies.

Goal: Create a lightweight, event-cycle-based micro-survey system (max 5 questions per touchpoint) that gives us measurable, comparable data across clients and over time — without overwhelming organisers.

2. Survey Model: Event-Cycle Based

Instead of one monthly survey for everyone, we trigger different micro-surveys at key moments in the event lifecycle. This means the questions are always relevant to what the organiser is actually doing RIGHT NOW.

Proposed Survey Touchpoints (4 surveys across the cycle)

#TouchpointWhen to triggerWho fills itDuration
S1Onboarding & Setup2–4 weeks after kickoff (once organiser has started configuring)Organiser self-serve (link shared by TAM) or TAM fills on call~3 min
S2Pre-Event2–3 weeks before the event goes liveOrganiser self-serve or TAM fills on call~3 min
S3Post-Event1–2 weeks after event ends (during or right after wash-up call)Organiser self-serve (link shared by TAM) or TAM fills on call~3 min
S4Renewal / Annual~1 month before contract renewal (or annually for multi-year)Organiser self-serve (link shared by TAM) or TAM fills on call~3 min

Why 4 touchpoints: Each maps to a different phase of the journey, captures different pain points, and avoids survey fatigue. Most clients will hit 2–3 per year (not all 4).

Ownership model: Ideally the organiser fills the survey themselves (TAM shares the link). If the organiser hasn't completed it, the TAM fills it on a call as a backup. Only one person interacts with each organiser about surveys — no RM involvement — to avoid confusion and conflicting outreach.

3. Proposed Questions Per Survey

Max 5 questions each. Mix of scored (1–10 or 1–5) + 1 open-ended. All questions designed to be measurable, comparable across clients, and trackable over time.

4. Form Tool — Research & Recommendation

You need a tool that: (a) is quick to fill, (b) exports data cleanly for analysis, (c) can be embedded or linked easily, (d) ideally integrates with your dashboard/OKR tracking.

ToolProsConsCostVerdict
Google FormsFree, everyone knows it, auto-collects in Google Sheets, easy to shareLimited design, no conditional logic on free tier, no API for dashboardsFreeGood for Phase 1
TypeformBeautiful UX, conditional logic, API for dashboard integration, conversational formatPaid, company account needed (you already discussed with Susan)~$25/moBest for Phase 2
Tally.soFree (unlimited forms), Notion-like interface, conditional logic, integrations (Zapier, webhooks), embeddableLess known, no PlanHat integration nativelyFree (Pro $29/mo)Strong alternative
PlanHat custom formsData stays in CRM, linked to client record, AI analysis built-inSusan needs to check if this capability exists — unknownIncludedCheck with Susan
Router.soDeveloper-focused form handler and lead routerNOT a survey tool — no conditional logic, no survey analytics dashboard, no response visualisation. Designed for routing form submissions to sales reps, not collecting structured feedback.Free tier availableNot suitable
Recommendation: Start with Tally.so (free tier). It has conditional logic, clean export to Google Sheets, Notion-like editing, embeddable links, and webhook integrations — all features Google Forms lacks. It's free for unlimited forms and responses. Move to PlanHat long-term if Susan confirms it has survey/custom form capability (keeps data in CRM, linked to client records). Router.so is not suitable — it's a developer-focused lead routing tool, not a survey platform.
